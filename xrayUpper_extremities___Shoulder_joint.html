Shoulder Girdle & Joint (6 bones)

Clavicle

Location: Collarbone

Function: Connects arm to trunk, brace for shoulder

Joints: Sternoclavicular, Acromioclavicular

Scapula

Location: Shoulder blade

Function: Attachment for shoulder muscles

Landmarks: Spine, Acromion, Coracoid

Humerus (Proximal)

Location: Upper arm bone

Function: Forms shoulder joint, attachment for arm muscles

Landmarks: Head, Neck, Greater/Lesser Tubercles

Acromioclavicular Joint

Type: Plane synovial joint

Function: Connects scapula to clavicle

Motion: Limited gliding movement

Sternoclavicular Joint

Type: Saddle synovial joint

Function: Connects clavicle to sternum

Motion: Elevation, depression, protraction, retraction

Glenohumeral Joint

Type: Ball-and-socket synovial joint

Function: Main shoulder joint, widest ROM

Stability: Rotator cuff muscles, ligaments

Elbow Region (Joint Formed by 3 Bone Ends)

Distal Humerus (Elbow Part)

Part of Upper Arm Bone:

• Trochlea: Spool-shaped, articulates with ulna

• Capitulum: Knob-like, articulates with radius

• Medial Epicondyle: "Funny bone" bump

• Lateral Epicondyle: Extensor muscle origin

This is just the bottom part of the humerus bone

Proximal Ulna (Elbow Part)

Top of Inner Forearm Bone:

• Olecranon Process: Forms elbow tip

• Coronoid Process: Anterior projection

• Trochlear Notch: C-shaped hinge groove

• Radial Notch: Articulates with radius

This is just the top part of the ulna bone

Proximal Radius (Elbow Part)

Top of Outer Forearm Bone:

• Radial Head: Disc-shaped, rotates

• Radial Neck: Constriction below head

• Radial Tuberosity: Biceps tendon attachment

• Annular Ligament: Stabilizes radial head

This is just the top part of the radius bone

Elbow Joint Itself

NOT separate bones!

A Joint Where 3 Bone Ends Meet:

• Distal Humerus (bottom part)

• Proximal Ulna (top part)

• Proximal Radius (top part)

3 Articulations:

• Humeroulnar (hinge)

• Humeroradial (pivot)

• Proximal Radioulnar (pivot)

Forearm Region (2 Long Bones)

Radius (Shaft)

Location: Lateral forearm bone

Function: Rotates around ulna

Movement: Supination/pronation

Distal End: Articulates with carpal bones

Ulna (Shaft)

Location: Medial forearm bone

Function: Stable reference bone

Distal End: Styloid process, triangular fibrocartilage

Note: Does not directly articulate with carpal bones

Wrist Region (8 Carpal Bones)

Proximal Carpal Row

Bones (4):

• Scaphoid: Most lateral, articulates with radius

• Lunate: Crescent-shaped, articulates with radius

• Triquetrum: Pyramid-shaped, articulates with TFCC

• Pisiform: Sesamoid bone in flexor carpi ulnaris

Distal Carpal Row

Bones (4):

• Trapezium: Saddle joint with 1st metacarpal

• Trapezoid: Smallest carpal, wedge-shaped

• Capitate: Largest carpal, head articulates with lunate

• Hamate: Has hook (hamulus) for ligament attachment

Wrist Joint Complex

Type: Condyloid synovial joint

Main Joint: Radiocarpal joint

Movements: Flexion/Extension, Radial/Ulnar deviation

Stability: Multiple ligaments, triangular fibrocartilage complex

Hand & Fingers (19 bones)

Metacarpals (5)

Location: Palm bones

Numbering: I (thumb) to V (little finger)

Joints: Carpometacarpal (CMC), Metacarpophalangeal (MCP)

Special: 1st metacarpal (thumb) has saddle CMC joint

Phalanges (14)

Location: Finger bones

Segments:

• Proximal phalanges (5)

• Middle phalanges (4) - thumb has none

• Distal phalanges (5)

Joints: PIP, DIP joints (hinge type)

Thumb (Pollex)

Unique Features:

• Only 2 phalanges (others have 3)

• Saddle CMC joint allows opposition

• Wider range of motion

• Muscles: Thenar eminence, adductor pollicis
